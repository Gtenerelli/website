    Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects How does GDPR impact digital projects?


    All software should be written to comply is local and global laws and guidelines, this often means writing code to defend confidential or sensitive information such as names, addresses, credit card or bank account information. Information should also not be kept longer than it is required, and code should be designed to reflect this requirement.
    GDPR is a regulation that requires businesses to protect the personal data and privacy of EU citizens for transactions that occur within EU member states. And non-compliance could cost companies dearly. f these guidelines are not followed and there is a data breach, then potentially confidential information could fall into the wrong hands causing issues for customers and the company. Such leaks are often featured in the news, examples include the leak for 150,000 confidential NHS records due to a coding error. These breaches are a problem not only the company/organization the caused the leak but also for the subjects of the data leak. This information can be used to exploit people and can cause harassment and harm. The company/organization also opens itself to negative feedback and potentially the loss of business and could also be subject to large fines. REF
